Local Analysis of ODEs (Second-Order Linear)

Consider the general homogeneous second-order linear ODE:
                                         y + P (x)y + Q(x)y = 0.

The general solution is a linear combination of two linearly independent solutions y1(x)
and y2(x):

                                         y(x) = c1y1(x) + c2y2(x).

Classification of Points x0

   1. Ordinary Point x0: P (x) and Q(x) are both analytic at x0, meaning they have
       convergent Taylor expansions around x0.

   2. Regular Singular Point x0: The point is not ordinary, but the following conditions
       hold:

          ∑ (x - x0)P (x) is analytic at x0 (i.e., P (x) behaves no worse than 1/(x - x0) near
             x0).

          ∑ (x - x0)2Q(x) is analytic at x0 (i.e., Q(x) behaves no worse than 1/(x - x0)2
             near x0).

       At a regular singular point, the ODE admits solutions of the Frobenius form:

                                                                                  

                                            y(x) = an(x - x0)n+,

                                                                                 n=0

       where  is the indicial root (a real or complex constant).

   3. Irregular Singular Point: An irregular singular point satisfies neither the ordi-
       nary nor the regular singular conditions. There is no general standard method for
       solving ODEs around irregular singular points.

    For ODEs around ordinary points, we expand y(x) in a Taylor series and match
coefficients of different powers of x - x0. For regular singular points, we use the Frobenius
expansion.

                              xny + xy + y = 0.

∑ If n = 1: P (x) = 1, Q(x) = 1/x. x = 0 is a regular singular point.

∑ If n = 2: P (x) = 1/x, Q(x) = 1/x2. x = 0 is a regular singular point.
